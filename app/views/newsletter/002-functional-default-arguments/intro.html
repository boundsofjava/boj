Welcome to the second issue of The Bounds of Java Newsletter, written after a wonderful winter vacation with the whole family. This year, we went to Bariloche, a lovely city located in the south-west of Argentina.

According to Wikipedia, San Carlos de Bariloche is a city in the province of Río Negro, Argentina, situated in the foothills of the Andes mountain range on the southern shores of Nahuel Huapi Lake. After development of extensive public works and Alpine-styled architecture, the city emerged in the 1930s and 1940s as a major tourism centre with skiing, trekking and mountaineering facilities. In addition, it has numerous restaurants, cafés, and chocolate shops.

I must add that it has quite a lot of independent, top-quality breweries as well. We all spent a great time there, especially the kids, who won't forget this holiday for the rest of their lives.
